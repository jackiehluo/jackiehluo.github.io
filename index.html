About Me

Hi, there! My name is Jackie Luo, and I'm a junior at Columbia University studying computer science and philosophy. Back in high school, I was a fashion photographer, founder and editor-in-chief of ACHE Magazine, and founder and executive director of Fashion Cares. Now, I'm part of the Columbia Organization of Rising Entrepreneurs (CORE) and ADI Labs, a student-led initiative to build open-source projects for the Columbia community.

In CORE, I launched Ignite@CU for undergraduate designers, developers, and entrepreneurs in New York City, with talks from companies like Shapeways, Fifty-Three, Bonobos, and more. I also led design and marketing for #StartupColumbia and the #StartupColumbia Challenge, the university's first annual entrepreneurship festival and venture competition with a $50,000 prize pool for students. Finally, I ran the Silicon Valley tech trek, bringing a group of students to the Bay Area to visit companies like Asana, Coinbase, KPCB, Palantir, Sequoia Capital, Sift Science, and Square.

I'm in San Francisco for the spring to work on fixing email with Nylas, and I'll be on the applied machine learning team at Apple this summer. Last summer, I was an engineering intern at Percolate, designing and implementing a mobile automation framework for five iOS and Android apps.

Projects


Liongram

Liongram is a campus candygram service that I started with a group of friends. We took orders from students at Columbia and delivered candygrams to dorm rooms of their choices. To track the pending orders and their statuses (placed, paid, delivered), I built a web app in Rails that allowed us to assign deliveries to people and automatically notify buyers as the orders progressed through the system.


Sensei

Sensei is a HackMIT project that tracks movement in security cameras using video processing algorithms to bring analytics to real-world stores. We built a motion detector in MATLAB that analyzes movement of people entering places to gain valuable data, like how many people are visiting, how long they are staying, and what the conversion rate of people in the store is. We also used Clarifai's training API to identify unique visitors through facial recognition, captured through consistent movement in the motion detector. Sensei won third place overall and GE’s award for Most Innovative Use of Data.


Sentiment

I worked on an ADI Labs team of four to build Sentiment, an app that analyzes the sentiment at Columbia based on students' online comments. As a back-end developer, I created a web scraper to get all comments on Bwog, a popular campus publication, for the last day and run it through sentiment analysis. The SQL database stores information for each day, like the top comments for that week and the sentiment at that point in time. We're currently working on mapping changes in mood onto major events on campus.


No New Friends

I developed an app to connect strangers at Columbia over coffee. Inspired by Tea with Strangers, No New Friends gives students the opportunity to meet people outside of their normal circles. The app is built in Flask and implements user registration and login with a PostgreSQL database and the Facebook Graph API for importing profile pictures. Users must have @columbia.edu email addresses to register and can then view each others' profiles and social media links and contact each other.
