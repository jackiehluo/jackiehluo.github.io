About Me

Hi, there! My name is Jackie Luo, and I'm a junior at Columbia University studying computer science and philosophy. I'm on the board of the Columbia Organization of Rising Entrepreneurs (CORE) and part of ADI Labs, a student-led initiative to build open-source projects for the Columbia community.

In CORE, I launched Ignite@CU for undergraduate designers, developers, and entrepreneurs in New York City, with talks from companies like Shapeways, Fifty-Three, Bonobos, and more. I also led design and marketing for #StartupColumbia and the #StartupColumbia Challenge, the university's first annual entrepreneurship festival and venture competition with a $50,000 prize pool for students. Finally, I ran the Silicon Valley tech trek, where I brought a group of students to the Bay Area to visit companies like Asana, Coinbase, KPCB, Palantir, Sequoia Capital, Sift Science, and Square.

I'm going to be in San Francisco in the spring to intern with Nylas, working to fix email. Over the summer, I was an engineering intern at Percolate, designing and implementing a mobile automation framework for five iOS and Android apps. I was also selected as one of twenty women to attend Square Code Camp, a week-long immersion program with coding workshops, leadership sessions, and a hackathon.

Projects


Sensei

Sensei is a HackMIT project that tracks movement in security cameras using video processing algorithms to bring analytics to real-world stores. We built a motion detector in MATLAB that analyzes movement of people entering places to gain valuable data, like how many people are visiting, how long they are staying, and what the conversion rate of people in the store is. We also used Clarifai's training API to identify unique visitors through facial recognition, captured through consistent movement in the motion detector. Sentiment

I worked on an ADI Labs team of four to build Sentiment, an app that analyzes the sentiment at Columbia based on students' online comments. As a back-end developer, I created a web scraper to get all comments on Bwog, a popular campus publication, for the last day and run it through sentiment analysis. The SQL database stores information for each day, like the top comments for that week and the sentiment at that point in time. We're currently working on mapping changes in mood onto major events on campus. You can view the project on GitHub.


No New Friends

I developed an app to connect strangers at Columbia over coffee. Inspired by Tea with Strangers, No New Friends is meant to give students who want to meet people outside of their normal circles the opportunity. The app is built in Flask and implements user registration and login with a PostgreSQL database and the Facebook Graph API for importing profile pictures. Users must have @columbia.edu email addresses to register and can then view each others' profiles and social media links and contact each other. No New Friends is currently deployed on Heroku, and you can view it on GitHub.
